1. An electric bulb, also known as a light bulb.
2. Its a device that produces light when electricity is passed through its terminals.
3. The bulb has two thick wires that are connected in the center by a thin wire called a filament.
4. When an electric current passes through the filament, it heats up and glows, producing light.
5. A small amount of inert gas is present in the air inside the glass bulb to prevent the filament from becoming too hot and breaking.
6. If the filament breaks, the bulb is said to be fused.

What is the function of a bulb?

A light-bulb produces light from electricity. In addition to lighting a dark space, they can be used to show an electronic device is on, to direct traffic, for heat, and for many other purposes. Billions are in use, some even in outer space.
